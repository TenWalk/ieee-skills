# IEEE Cover Letter Templates

## New submission

```text
Dear Editor [Name],

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "[Title]" for consideration in [Journal/Special
Issue]. The manuscript addresses [problem] in [system/scope], which is central to [journal-relevant
area].

The main contribution is [one-sentence contribution]. In contrast to [prior approach/category],
the proposed [method/analysis/system] [technical distinction]. The manuscript supports this claim
through [evidence 1], [evidence 2], and [evidence 3], including [headline result if real and
author supplied].

We believe the work is well suited to [Journal] because [scope/fit sentence]. The manuscript is
original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. [Add required
data/code/ethics/conflict statements if supplied.]

Thank you for considering our submission. We look forward to your response.

Sincerely,
[Authors]
```

## Revised submission

```text
Dear Editor [Name],

We thank you and the reviewers for the careful assessment of our manuscript, "[Title]"
([Manuscript ID]). We have revised the manuscript in response to the comments and submit the
revised version for further consideration.

The major revisions are: (1) [change + manuscript anchor], (2) [change + anchor], and (3) [change
+ anchor]. These changes [how they improve clarity/evidence/scope].

A detailed point-by-point response is provided in the response letter. Reviewer comments are quoted
there, and all manuscript changes are traceable to the revised text.

Thank you for considering our revised manuscript.

Sincerely,
[Authors]
```

## Transfer or resubmission after rejection

Do not hide the history if the platform asks for it. Frame what changed:

- "This manuscript is a substantially revised version of a prior submission to [venue], and the
  current version addresses [main issues]."
- "The manuscript has been reframed for [target journal] by emphasizing [fit] and adding
  [evidence]."

Only mention a previous rejection when required by the journal or useful for transparent transfer.

## Compliance statements

Use only supplied facts:

- Originality/exclusive submission: "This manuscript has not been published and is not under
  consideration elsewhere."
- Conflicts: "The authors declare no conflicts of interest." or supplied conflicts.
- Data/code: "The data/code supporting this work are available at [repository]" or "available upon
  reasonable request" only when true and acceptable.
- Suggested reviewers: include affiliation/email only if the user provides them and the journal asks.
- Opposed reviewers: give a factual conflict reason, not a personal critique.

## Tone checks

1. Does the first paragraph name the target and the technical fit?
2. Is the contribution specific enough to distinguish the paper from a generic abstract?
3. Are all quantitative claims real author-supplied results?
4. Are compliance statements true and not guessed?
5. Is the letter shorter than the paper's Introduction?
